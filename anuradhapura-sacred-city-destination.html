Anuradhapura Sacred City

Ancient capital and UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring massive stupas, the sacred Bodhi tree, and profound spiritual heritage.

Buddhist Religious Destination

Explore Ancient Spirituality in Anuradhapura

Journey to Sri Lanka's first capital and one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities. Anuradhapura Sacred City showcases 2,500 years of Buddhist civilization with massive stupas, ancient temples, and the sacred Bodhi tree.

About Anuradhapura Sacred City:

Anuradhapura served as Sri Lanka's capital for over 1,300 years and represents one of the world's most significant archaeological and spiritual sites. This UNESCO World Heritage city contains the largest collection of ruins from a single period in Sri Lankan history, spanning from the 4th century BC to the 11th century AD.

The sacred city is home to the Sri Maha Bodhi tree, grown from a cutting of the original Bodhi tree under which Lord Buddha attained enlightenment. This 2,300-year-old tree is considered the oldest historically documented tree in the world and remains a powerful pilgrimage site for Buddhists globally.

What Makes Anuradhapura Special:

The city features some of the world's largest and most ancient Buddhist stupas, including Ruwanwelisaya, Thuparamaya (the oldest stupa in Sri Lanka), and Abhayagiri, which was once among the tallest structures in the ancient world. These magnificent monuments showcase the architectural and engineering prowess of ancient Sri Lankan civilization.

  • Venerating the sacred Sri Maha Bodhi tree
  • Exploring massive ancient stupas and monasteries
  • Discovering archaeological treasures and ruins
  • Learning about ancient hydraulic engineering
  • Experiencing living Buddhist heritage
Best Time to Visit

Anuradhapura can be visited year-round, but the cooler months from November to March offer the most comfortable conditions for exploring the extensive archaeological site. Early morning visits (6:00-9:00 AM) and late afternoon (4:00-6:00 PM) provide the best lighting and cooler temperatures for photography and exploration.

Plan for a full day to properly explore the major sites. Wear modest clothing covering shoulders and legs, comfortable walking shoes, and bring sun protection as much of the site is exposed to direct sunlight.

  • UNESCO World Heritage site with 2,500 years of continuous Buddhist civilization
  • Home to the world's oldest historically documented tree - the sacred Sri Maha Bodhi
  • Magnificent ancient stupas among the largest in the world from their era
